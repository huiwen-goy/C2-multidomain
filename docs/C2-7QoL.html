Satisfaction With Life Scale

Responses are seven discrete options, ranging from 1 (Strongly Disagree) to 7 (Strongly Agree). The overall score is an average of responses, with higher numbers indicating better life satisfaction. No items are reverse-coded.

WHOQOL-BREF

back to top

Some items use 5-point Likert scales while others use ordinal scales.

Two items are analyzed individually: overall perception of quality of life, and overall perception of health. The remaining questions are clustered into four domains: Physical health, psychological health, social relationships, and environment. Three items are reverse-coded. Responses within each domain are averaged to create mean domain scores, which are then multiplied by 4 to make domain scores comparable with scores from the WHOQOL-100. Higher scores indicate a better outcome.

A domain have up to 1-2 missing items per participant, as specified in the WHOQOL-BREF scoring procedure. The average domain score is used as a substitute in place of the missing response(s). More missing items than that means that the domain score for that participant is invalid. If the participants is missing >20% of their data overall, their overall WHOQOL-BREF score is invalid.
